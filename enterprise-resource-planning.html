Frequently Asked Questions

There are many benefits of using the ERP system, but some basic features that every ERP system should have are -
  • Efficiency & Productivity: ERP Automates tasks, streamlines workflows, and eliminates data silos.
  • Visibility & Reporting: ERP enables central data repository enabling real-time insights which helps in decision-making.
  • Cost Savings: ERP system controls inventory, optimizes purchasing, and enhances financial management.
  • Collaboration: ERP is a centralized platform which improves departments communication and collaboration.
  • Customer Service: ERP enables faster responses to inquiries and efficient order fulfillment of customers.

ERP systems are highly customizable, and can adapt themselves with any business. Following are some of the pointers on how ERP systems can fit with any business -
  • Configurability: ERP system adjusts settings and workflows without altering code.
  • Modularity: Businesses can choose and implement modules based on specific needs.
  • Customization Tools: Businesses can modify functionality, create custom reports, and integrate third-party apps.
  • Cost & Complexity: ERP balances out the benefits against potential drawbacks of extensive customization.

Below are some of the challenges that business can face while implementing the ERP system -
  • Resistance to Change: Staff may feel reluctant to adapt to the new processes & technology.
  • Data Migration: Complex data transfer from legacy systems, may result in risking errors.
  • Executive Support: It is vital for ERP success, lacking it may limit resources and authority.
  • Lack of Executive Support: Insufficient resources and authority hinder success.

The time it takes to construct an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system varies greatly based on a number of criteria, including project complexity, organizational size, level of customization required, and resource availability. Overall, it takes time to build an ERP system, so It's essential to allocate sufficient time and resources to each phase of the project to ensure a successful ERP implementation.
